
FinSight
AI-Driven Financial Health Scoring for SMEs
Product Requirements Document  ·  v1.0
Hackathon Submission — BIT Sindri


1. Executive Summary
FinSight is a web-based financial health scoring tool for small and medium enterprises. It accepts a GST return PDF or a basic P&L statement, uses AI to extract structured financial data from it, computes a set of core health indicators, and presents the SME owner with a plain-language health score, a profit margin trend chart, and a short list of prioritized improvement actions.

The product is not built for banks. It is built for the SME owner who has no CFO, no financial analyst, and no visibility into whether their business is deteriorating until it is too late. The core insight is that over 90% of GST-registered Indian SMEs do not maintain formal balance sheets, but almost all file GST returns and have bank transaction data — making PDF extraction the most practical data entry point.


2. Problem Statement
The Real Gap
Existing credit scoring tools are built for lenders — they score the SME after the fact. What is missing is a tool that serves the SME owner in real time, giving them forward-looking visibility into their own financial health before a cash crunch, before a loan rejection, before it is too late.

3. Target Users
Primary User
SME Owner / Proprietor —  SME owner or proprietor running a GST-registered business with annual turnover between ₹20L and ₹5Cr. Likely has limited financial literacy. Files GST returns quarterly or monthly. Has no in-house finance team.

Secondary Users
Bank or NBFC Field Officer —  Loan officers or relationship managers evaluating a business client before onboarding.
Early-Stage Founder —  Startup founders or micro-enterprise operators building a pitch or seeking early-stage funding.

For the hackathon, primary focus remains on the SME owner. The secondary users validate that the output is legible and useful to non-technical evaluators.

6. Core Features
6.1  PDF Ingestion & AI Extraction
Accepts GSTR-3B, GSTR-1, or a basic P&L statement in PDF format.
Claude API (claude-sonnet-4) reads the document and extracts: taxable turnover, output tax, ITC claimed, expenses, net profit, and reporting period.
Structured output validated against a schema before scoring begins. Extraction errors are surfaced to the user with guidance on which fields are missing.

6.2  Financial Health Score
Composite score from 0 to 100, computed from four weighted indicator groups.

Indicator Group
Metrics Included
Weight
Profitability
Gross margin %, net margin %, revenue growth MoM
30%
Liquidity Proxy
Revenue-to-tax ratio, ITC utilization rate
25%
Expense Health
Expense-to-revenue ratio, expense volatility
25%
Compliance Signal
Filing regularity, turnover consistency
20%


Score bands: 0–40 (High Risk), 41–65 (Watch), 66–85 (Stable), 86–100 (Healthy). Each band maps to a specific set of recommendations.

6.3  Profit Margin Trend Chart
Single bar/line chart showing net profit margin across available periods extracted from the uploaded document.
X-axis: reporting month or quarter. Y-axis: margin percentage.
Rendered using Recharts (React). No external charting service required.

6.4  Risk Flags & Suggestions
Up to 5 ranked flags, each tied to a specific metric threshold breach.
Example: 'Your expense ratio increased from 61% to 78% between Q2 and Q3. This is the primary driver of your score drop.'
Suggestions are rule-based for v1 — no generative output for suggestions to ensure consistency and auditability.

6.5  Summary Export
One-click PDF export of the health score, chart, and suggestions for the user to share with a bank officer or advisor.

7.3  Scoring Engine Logic
The scoring engine is a deterministic function — given the same input, it always returns the same score. No ML model is used in v1. This is intentional: it makes the system auditable, explainable, and deployable without training data.

Each indicator is normalized to a 0–100 scale against defined thresholds (e.g., net margin: 0% = 0 points, 20%+ = 100 points).
Weighted sum produces the composite score.
Flags are triggered when any single indicator falls below a critical threshold, regardless of the composite score.

ML-based anomaly detection (e.g., isolation forest on expense volatility) is scoped as a v2 enhancement once a dataset of SME submissions exists.

8. Constraints & Assumptions
PDF must be machine-readable (not a scanned image). Scanned PDF support via OCR is a v2 feature.
Minimum viable input: at least 2 periods of turnover data. Single-period uploads produce a score with a low-confidence flag.
All computation happens server-side or client-side. No user financial data is stored permanently in v1.
GSTR-3B is the primary supported format. GSTR-1 and basic P&L are secondary targets with slightly lower extraction confidence.
The product does not connect to GSTN, banking APIs, or any government system. All data flows from the user's own uploaded file.


9. Out of Scope for v1
Bank statement integration or GSTN API pull.
User accounts, history, or saved reports.
Multi-document comparison across companies.
ML-based predictive scoring or bankruptcy probability model.
Chatbot or conversational interface.
Mobile app (responsive web only).

Appendix — Key Financial Terms Used
Term
Definition
Net Margin
Net profit divided by total revenue, expressed as a percentage
ITC Utilization Rate
Input Tax Credit claimed as a proportion of output tax liability — a proxy for supply chain efficiency
Expense Ratio
Total operating expenses as a percentage of revenue — high ratios indicate cost pressure
Expense Volatility
Standard deviation of expense ratio across periods — high volatility signals operational instability
Compliance Signal
Regularity and consistency of GST filings — gaps or large turnover swings may indicate reporting issues
